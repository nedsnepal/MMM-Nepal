Who We Are

May Measure Month Nepal is led by the Nepal Development Society (NEDS) with support from the International Society of Hypertension We train research assistants, coordinate sites and trainings, conduct health camps and publish open data to improve hypertension awareness and control in Nepal.

Our Approach

  • Opportunistic screening in adults aged 18 and above
  • Mobile camps in public spaces and workplaces
  • Standardized BP measurement protocols
  • Data collection via nationwide surveillance
  • Referral pathways and counselling mechanisms for elevated readings

Collaborating Partners

  • International Society of Hypertension
  • Local hypertension societies
  • Community groups nationwide
  • Medical colleges and hospitals
  • Volunteer health workers

Campaign Archive

Our journey since 2017: expanding access to blood pressure screening across Nepal.

2017

International Society of Hypertension launches May Measurement Month worldwide.

2018

Nepal Development Society joins as national lead, establishing first screening sites.

2019

District coverage grows: Campaign expands to major districts with over 5,000 participants screened.

2020

Although MMM was not executed globally due to the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, Nepal has continued its legacy, building on the success of MMM in the past years. 11,348 screened despite COVID-19: Adapted protocols with safety measures; continued vital screening services.

2021

Hybrid virtual + in-person: Launched online training and telemedicine consultations alongside physical camps.

2022

13,120 screened across 32 districts: Major expansion reaching more rural and underserved communities.

2023

Targeting all 77 districts with enhanced data collection and referral systems.
